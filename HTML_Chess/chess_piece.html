CINS 465: Assignment 4 by Colin Ennis

Pawn - The most basic piece in Chess that acts as guards for your high value pieces. A pawn can move 1 or 2 spaces on its first move and only 1 thereafter. A pawn cannot go backwards and can only take other pieces diagonally. Pawns are valued at a +1.

Knight - Knights can move in two ways; vertically two spaces and horizontally one to the left or one to the right, and horinzontally two spaces and one vertically up or down. Knights can move in this L shape in any direction and can at certain spaces in the board have 8 total moves at any given moment. Knights are valued at a +3.

Bishop - Bishops are a strong piece that can move any amount of spaces diagonally. Depending on the state of the game Bishops are valued at a +3-3.5.

Rooks - Rooks are a high value piece that can move either horizontally or vertically any amount of spaces. Rooks are valued at a +5.

Queen - The strongest piece in Chess. A queen can move any direction any amount of spaces. Queens are valued at a +9.

King - The piece that needs protecting. Once a King has been check-mated the game is over. A king can move 1 space in any direction around its location.
